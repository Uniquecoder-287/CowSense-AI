1. Light Mode (Reference: Uploaded Images)
The Light Mode must replicate the current design seen in the screenshots, serving as the default theme.

Background: Predominantly White or Very Light Grey (e.g., #FFFFFF or a near-white shade).

Card/Container Backgrounds: Use a slightly darker shade of white or light grey than the main background to provide subtle depth and visual separation for elements like cow lists, notification cards, and vital boxes.

Text: High-contrast Dark Grey or Black for maximum readability.

Key Color Usage:

Health Indicators (e.g., the circular health score): Use the existing Green (Healthy), Orange (Needs Attention), and Red (Urgent) color palette. These colors must remain vibrant and pop against the light background.

Icons and Accent Colors: Maintain the current subtle blues/purples/teals for system elements (like the settings gear, alert bell, etc.).

2. Dark Mode (Goal: Pitch Dark/AMOLED Friendly)
The Dark Mode must use a deep, pitch-dark background to minimize eye strain and save battery on OLED/AMOLED screens.

Background: Pitch Black or an extremely dark near-black (e.g., #000000 or a very dark #121212).

Card/Container Backgrounds: Use a dark grey that is visibly lighter than the background but still very dark (e.g., #1F1F1F or similar). This is crucial for separating UI elements from the black background.

Text: White or Very Light Grey text (e.g., #F0F0F0) for optimal contrast and readability. Avoid pure white text on pitch black to prevent halation/bleeding effects; a slightly off-white is preferred.

Key Color Usage & Accessibility:

Health Indicators (Green, Orange, Red): Colors must be retained, but ensure they are vibrant enough to pass WCAG AA contrast standards against the dark background. They should remain instantly recognizable.

Accents: Desaturate existing accent colors slightly or add a luminous quality to ensure they don't appear overly bright or jarring.
